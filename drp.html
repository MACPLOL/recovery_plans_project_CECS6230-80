Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

A Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) is a formal document that details how an organization restores critical IT systems after events such as natural disasters, system failures, or cyberattacks.

Key Components:

  • Risk Assessment
  • Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) & Recovery Point Objectives (RPO)
  • Resource Inventory & Backup Procedures
  • Roles & Responsibilities
  • Testing & Updating

Importance to IT Auditing: Auditors ensure the DRP is well-documented, tested, and aligns with standards. They verify backups can be restored within the defined RTO and RPO.
