Incident Response Plan (IRP)

An Incident Response Plan (IRP) outlines how an organization prepares for, detects, contains, and recovers from security incidents, ranging from malware outbreaks to insider threats (TraceSecurity, 2023). The IRP aims to safeguard data confidentiality, integrity, and availability while minimizing legal and reputational harm.

According to CISA (2025), an effective IRP clarifies roles and responsibilities, provides guidelines for key activities, and ensures that the right people are engaged before, during, and after a suspected incident. Training and tabletop exercises are fundamental for ensuring incident readiness and improving organizational coordination.

Key Components:

  • Incident Detection & Monitoring: Systems like SIEM and IDS to flag anomalies.
  • Roles & Responsibilities: A dedicated incident response team authorized to take rapid containment measures.
  • Containment, Eradication, & Recovery: Steps to isolate affected systems, remove malicious code, and restore normal operations.
  • Communication & Escalation Procedures: Notification of executive leadership, regulators, and law enforcement (when appropriate).
  • Post-Incident Review & Lessons Learned: Blameless retrospectives that drive continuous improvement (CISA, 2025).

Importance to IT Auditing: From an audit perspective, the IRP must be consistently tested and updated in line with emerging threats. Auditors verify alignment with frameworks like NIST SP 800-61 and review whether escalation thresholds (who is contacted, and when) are well-defined (TraceSecurity, 2023). The IRP’s link to DRP and BCP is also crucial; if an incident escalates significantly, the organization may need to activate both disaster recovery measures and broader business continuity procedures.
